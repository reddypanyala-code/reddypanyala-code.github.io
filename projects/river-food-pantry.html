The River Food Pantry Warehouse Layout and Operations Design

Senior Capstone (ISyE 450) | 2025

Context

The River Food Pantry operates in a high-demand nonprofit environment where warehouse flow, inventory placement, and daily execution quality directly affect service reliability.

Problem

Existing layout and activity sequencing created movement inefficiencies, intermittent bottlenecks, and uneven use of available space during busy operating periods.

Methods

  • Mapped movement of materials and work activity through the warehouse process.
  • Analyzed operational bottlenecks and friction points across key workflow stages.
  • Evaluated layout alternatives based on practicality, space constraints, and execution clarity.

Outcome

Developed a set of realistic recommendations to improve layout structure, space utilization, and day-to-day workflow performance while aligning to organizational constraints. The project was awarded First Prize in the Ratner Senior Design Project Competition (Fall 2025).

My Role

Led analysis of movement and operations patterns, synthesized findings into implementable recommendations, and supported communication of changes for stakeholder decision making.
